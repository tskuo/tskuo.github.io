ABOUT

I am a M.S. student majoring in Electrical Engineering at Stanford University, where I am a research assistant in Stanford HCI Group and advised by Prof. James Landay.

I completed my B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering with Summa Cum Laude at National Taiwan University. I also received an Honorary Membership of Phi Tau Phi Scholastic Honor Society and an Irving T. Ho Memorial Fellowship. My undergraduate research spanned areas in signal processing, computer vision, and human-computer interaction. I was advised by Prof. Homer Chen (NTU EE), Prof. Yu-Chiang Frank Wang (NTU EE), Prof. Bing-Yu Robin Chen (NTU CSIE), and Prof. Xing-Dong Yang (Dartmouth College, CS). I have published five papers in these areas so far.

Beyond research, I was also the Chair of 2017 MakeNTU, the Founder of NTUEE MakerSpace, and the Director of the Academic Department in NTUEE Student Association.
