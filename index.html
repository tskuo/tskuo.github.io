Tzu-Sheng Kuo

I am a first-year PhD student in the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, co-advised by Prof. Ken Holstein and Prof. Haiyi Zhu. I develop interactive systems and participatory design methods that support online and offline communities to build, maintain, and evaluate AI systems. I am particularly interested in shaping future AI technology that reflects community stakeholders' values (for example, around fairness, accountability, and transparency).

Previously, I graduated with an M.S. from Stanford University, where I worked with Prof. James Landay and Prof. Elizabeth Murnane in the Stanford HCI Group. I also graduated summa cum laude from National Taiwan University, where I received B.S. in Electrical Engineering.
