Tzu-Sheng Kuo

I am a rising second-year PhD student in the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, co-advised by Prof. Ken Holstein and Prof. Haiyi Zhu. I develop interactive systems and design methods to support diverse stakeholder participation in the development, maintenance, and evaluation of AI systems. My current research focuses on building tools that empower community stakeholders to shape the behavior of ML models via dataset curation. In the long run, my research aims to democratize the AI development process in order to better incorporate community stakeholders' values.

Previously, I graduated with an M.S. from Stanford University, where I worked with Prof. James Landay and Prof. Elizabeth Murnane in the Stanford HCI Group. I also graduated summa cum laude from National Taiwan University, where I received B.S. in Electrical Engineering.
